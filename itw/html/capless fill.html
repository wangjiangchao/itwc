We are the pioneer.

ITW is the original inventor of capless refuel system. Additionally, we are the inventor of Mis-Fuel Inhibitor, an integrated system which biases Petrol and Diesel fuel. With 15 years of Capless Refuel R&D and production experiences under our belt, we hold #1 position by both number of capless systems produced (>5 million units), and the by the number of geographies serviced (Europe, Asia, North America and South America).

Enhancing passenger experiences.

Simplicity is the ultimate sophistication. Years of focused R&D and engineering have enabled us to eliminate a car component which has been around since the 20s: the very gas cap itself. Now, hands and gloves stay clean at the gas station. The overall refuel is faster with pipe-in and pipe-out. Weak and arthritic customers no longer need to worry about the motion of twisting a cap. And this is just the beginning.

Reducing in-field warranties.

“Check Engine Light” warranty issues as a result of improper gas cap closure is completely eliminated, while lost or broken caps are no longer of any concern. We have plenty of data too to demonstrate before and after capless results (just ask).

Helping the environment

Fuel vapor emission regulations are becoming more stringent. Whether it’s EU’s Euro 6, USA’s LEV III or China’s Jing 6, our R&D centers around the world are working to ensure our Capless Refueling systems are engineered to meet the most stringent carbon emission requirements. Our patented sealing system achieves superb vapor sealing. Because the capless system is only open during a fuel gun nozzle insertion, we are eliminating a very large portion of the vapor emission which otherwise would have been exposed to the atmosphere with a gas cap. On top of it all, our double door system guards additional harmful vapor emissions for our environment.

A comprehensive product portfolio

Our family of capless solutions are designed to meet geography and OEM specifications, ranging from fuel pipe diameter, pipe material composition, onboard refueling vapor recovery (ORVR) requirements, and emission regulations.
